## Are Onions Safe for Dogs?

Veterinary organisations, including the ASPCA and Pet Poison Helpline, classify **onions as toxic to dogs**. Onions contain sulfur-containing compounds that can damage red blood cells, resulting in haemolytic anaemia.  
Source: https://www.petpoisonhelpline.com/poison/onions/

The Merck Veterinary Manual explains that toxicity can occur from raw, cooked, or powdered onion, and that onion powder found in processed foods is a particularly common source of exposure.  
Source: https://www.merckvetmanual.com/toxicology/food-hazards/onion-and-garlic-toxicity-in-animals

VCA Hospitals notes that clinical signs may not appear for several days after ingestion, but damage to red blood cells begins earlier.  
Source: https://vcahospitals.com/know-your-pet/onion-toxicity-in-dogs

## Why Onions Can Be a Problem for Some Dogs

### Main compounds of concern
- **N-propyl disulfide**, identified in veterinary toxicology sources as the compound responsible for damaging red blood cells.  
- **Thiosulphates**, also contributing to oxidative damage.

### Portion size and dog size
Toxicity is dose-dependent, but even moderate amounts can cause problems. Smaller dogs may be affected more severely by the same amount of onion compared to larger dogs.

### Preparation differences
Veterinary organisations classify **all forms of onion as unsafe**, including:
- Raw onions  
- Cooked onions  
- Onion powder (common in many processed foods)  
- Dehydrated onions  
- Onion-containing dishes such as soups, gravies, or sauces

## Signs Reported When Dogs React Poorly to Onions

- Vomiting  
- Diarrhoea  
- Lethargy  

### More serious signs
- Pale gums  
- Increased heart rate  
- Weakness or collapse  
- Signs consistent with haemolytic anaemia  

These signs are documented in toxicology summaries by VCA Hospitals and Merck Veterinary Manual.

## Ingredients or Additives to Watch For

- ❌ **Onion powder** (common in gravies, seasonings, snacks)  
- ❌ **Mixed dishes containing onions** (soups, stews, stir-fries)  
- ⚠️ **Other allium vegetables** (garlic, leeks, chives — also classified as toxic)

## Puppies vs. Adult Dogs

Puppies and adult dogs are both vulnerable, but puppies may be more severely affected due to their smaller size. Veterinary organisations caution that **no form of onion is safe for dogs at any age**.

## Other Common Variants of Onions

### Onion Powder
Considered particularly concerning because it is concentrated and commonly found in processed foods.

### Cooked Onions
Still toxic, as cooking does **not** remove harmful compounds.

## Dog-Friendly Alternatives Mentioned in Pet-Nutrition Sources

- Carrot sticks  
- Blueberries  
- Plain pumpkin purée  
- Commercial dog treats  

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

> *This FAQ summarises themes appearing in veterinary pet-care articles.  
> It is informational only and not a substitute for professional veterinary care.*

### Why are onions toxic to dogs?
Onions contain compounds that damage red blood cells, leading to haemolytic anaemia.

### Are cooked onions safer than raw onions?
No. Veterinary toxicology sources state that all forms of onion are unsafe.

### How soon do signs appear?
Signs may appear within a day but often develop over several days as red blood cell damage progresses.
